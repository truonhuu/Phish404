Tips to Recognize Phishing:

  • Check the sender's email domain carefully.
  • Be cautious if the email demands urgent action or threatens consequences.
  • Watch for unusual attachments or file types.
  • Avoid entering sensitive information via email links; instead, go to the official site directly.
  • Check for grammar errors or awkward language in professional emails.
  • Do not trust logos or official-looking designs at face value.
  • Always verify suspicious emails with your IT team or the company’s support centre.
  • Look for generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your name.
  • If it feels off, it probably is – trust your instincts!

Stay alert. Stop. Think twice before you click!

Tips to Avoid Vishing:

  • Never share PINs, passwords, or one-time codes over the phone.
  • Don’t trust caller ID – phone numbers can be spoofed.
  • If they claim to be from your bank or work, hang up and call back using the official number.
  • Be skeptical of urgent threats (like “your account will be locked”).
  • Ask the caller to send a written request if you’re unsure.
  • Don’t be rushed – real services won’t push you to act immediately.
  • Never give out personal information to unsolicited callers.
  • If you’re feeling pressured, simply end the call.
  • Discuss suspicious calls with someone you trust.
  • Keep your phone software up to date for security.

Stay alert. Stop. Think twice before sharing!
